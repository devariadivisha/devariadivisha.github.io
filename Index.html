Academic — IIT Kanpur

Predicting Competitive Bid Price for Infrastructure Projects Grade: A · Capstone
MBA 944 · Project I · Supervised by Dr. Veena Bansal · IIT Kanpur, 2024

Built a machine learning model to predict the awarded bid percentage for infrastructure projects in India, enabling data-driven Go/No-Go decisions. Used my own company's live bidding data — 120 projects (2020–2024) across highways, railways, and structures bid under NHAI, MoRTH, and state departments. Performed EDA, feature engineering, and implemented Linear Regression, Ridge Regression, and KNN. Ridge Regression achieved test R² = 0.677. The model accounts for terrain, project scale, contract mode (EPC/HAM/Item Rate), pavement type, and regional inflation. Average awarded bid was 17.4% below authority cost — a finding with direct strategic implications.

Python Ridge Regression KNN Feature Engineering Infrastructure Bidding Real Industry Data
This project is unique because the dataset came directly from live bids — not a public dataset. It bridges infrastructure domain knowledge with applied ML in a way few academic projects do.

Bachelor's Research

PPP Construction Contracts — Financial Constraints & Mitigation B.Tech Thesis · 2019
CEPT University · Guide: Mr. Kannan Rayappan · Co-Guide: Prof. Reshma Shah

Original research on why BOT-mode highway projects fail financially in India. Studied 6 real concession contracts (NHAI, GSRDC, MPRDC clients; EPC costs ₹87 Cr to ₹1,784 Cr) through personal interviews and financial model reconstruction. Built initial financial models with IRR, DSCR, and equity/debt analysis. Quantified losses across four constraint categories: land impediments (72% of total loss), toll revenue shortfall (11%), scope change disputes (14%), and policy changes (3%). Found that land impediments alone cause 20–31% project cost overrun. Proposed mitigation recommendations per constraint type.

Thesis research

PPP Construction Contracts: Financial Constraints & Suggestions for Mitigation Final Year Thesis · 2019
Guide: Mr. Kannan Rayappan · Co-Guide: Prof. Reshma Shah · Presented May 2019

Studied 6 real BOT highway concession projects (NHAI, GSRDC, MPRDC) ranging from ₹104 Cr to ₹1,971 Cr total project cost, involving concessionaires such as Valecha, Essel, Atlanta, and Oriental. Data collected through personal interviews and financial model reconstruction. Quantified losses by constraint category across all 6 cases and derived model percentage loss figures. Found land impediments (72% of total loss) as the dominant cause of PPP financial failure. Recommended pre-bid land availability thresholds, realistic traffic study protocols, and schedule compliance mechanisms.
